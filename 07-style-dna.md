# Universal HBR Style DNA Meta-Prompt

> Append this block to any chart-specific prompt to invoke the full Harvard Business Review editorial aesthetic.

---

## Style DNA Block

```text
STYLE: Harvard Business Review Data & Visuals editorial style.

LAYOUT:
- White background, no chart border or bounding box
- Bold declarative serif title (2 lines max), dark charcoal #222222
- 1–2 sentence sans-serif subtitle in medium gray #666666
- Sub-label or axis descriptor in bold sans-serif, smaller
- HBR shield watermark, bottom-right corner
- Source attribution: small italic gray, bottom-left
- All chart internals: clean sans-serif typography only

COLORS (use sparingly — max 2-3 per chart):
- HBR Red: #D0021B (urgency, bold trends, negative)
- HBR Purple/Violet: #7B5EA7 (featured/strategy)
- Teal/Sage: #3AADA5 (positive, HIGH value)
- Amber/Gold: #F5A623 (moderate, warning)
- Muted Blue: #89CFF0 (secondary, ghost/remainder)
- Warm Green: #4CAF50 (positive comparison)
- Orange: #E87722 (third series, expert)
- Near-black: #222222 (text, dark boxes)

CHART RULES:
- Dotted horizontal gridlines only (4–5 max), no vertical grid
- No axis border boxes or chart frames
- Direct data labels on elements (no separate legends if avoidable)
- Ghost/reference bars in pale wash of primary color (15–20% opacity)
- Flat fills only — no gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D
- Axis accent labels in matching color (e.g., teal for positive axis)
- Category tags above/below chart in colored small-caps
```
