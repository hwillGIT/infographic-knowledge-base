# Typography Reference

> HBR uses a serif headline + sans-serif body system, applied very consistently across all charts.

---

## Font System Overview

**Key Rule**: Titles use a distinctive, high-contrast serif. Everything inside the chart is sans-serif. There is zero decoration or drop shadows.

- **Headline font**: Georgia-like or custom serif (bold, high contrast)
- **Body / chart font**: Clean sans-serif (similar to Helvetica Neue or Inter)

---

## Element-by-Element Typography

| Element | Style | Size | Weight | Color |
|---------|-------|------|--------|-------|
| **Chart title** | Serif (Georgia-like), mixed case | 18–24px | Bold | Dark charcoal `#222222` |
| **Subtitle / explainer** | Sans-serif, regular weight | 13–15px | Regular | Medium gray `#666666` |
| **Sub-chart label** | Sans-serif, ALL CAPS small | 11–13px | Bold / Semi-bold | Accent color (teal or purple) |
| **Axis labels** | Sans-serif | 10–12px | Light | Gray `#888888` |
| **Data callouts / large numbers** | Sans-serif | 14–18px | Bold | Placed directly on data elements |
| **Source line** | Sans-serif, italic | 9–10px | Regular italic | Medium gray, bottom-left |
| **Category tags** | Sans-serif, small | 10–12px | Regular | Colored accent (teal = "Generative AI", purple = "Talent") |
| **Quadrant headers** | Sans-serif, centered | 14–16px | Bold | White or dark on colored background |
| **Quadrant descriptors** | Sans-serif, italic | 11–13px | Italic | Directly below headers |
| **Legend labels** | Sans-serif | 11–12px | Bold | Matching series color |
| **Inline emphasis text** | Sans-serif | Body size | Regular | Accent color (teal, purple, orange) |

## Title Conventions

### Declarative Titles
Every chart title **states the conclusion**, not just the variable:

| Wrong (Descriptive) | Correct (Declarative) |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| "Employee Engagement by Region" | "Low Employee Engagement Across the Globe" |
| "CEO vs. Employee Pay" | "Rising CEO-Employee Pay Gap" |
| "GLP-1 Consumer Data" | "Consumer Interest in GLP-1s" |
| "Board Performance Metrics" | "Human Board Performance" |

### Title Formatting Rules
- Maximum 2 lines
- Mixed case (not all caps)
- Dark charcoal color `#222222`
- Bold weight
- Serif typeface
- No punctuation at end (no periods)

---

## Subtitle Conventions

- 1–3 sentences of interpretive framing
- Sets context for what the data shows
- Sans-serif, regular weight
- Medium gray color
- Never restates the title — adds new information

---

## Special Typography Techniques

### Inline Colored Text
Inside chart bodies (especially tables and 2×2s), selected words are colored in the accent color while surrounding text stays black or gray. Creates editorial emphasis without bolding entire blocks.

### Hero Column Italic
In comparison tables, the featured/recommended column uses italic text throughout, while others remain roman.

### Direct Data Labels
Numeric values are placed directly on or immediately adjacent to data elements (bars, dots, bubbles). No separate legend box if avoidable.

### Axis Accent Labels
Axis labels appear in the chart's accent color (e.g., teal, purple) rather than black. Often paired with an italic sub-question.
